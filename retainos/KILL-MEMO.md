# RetainOS Kill Memo

Status: Killed
Date: 2026-03-10
Decision: No-go

## Decision

Do not build RetainOS as a standalone hosted cancellation-flow product.

## Why We Killed It

The market already has too much coverage on the obvious wedge.

Existing vendors already cover most of the surface area we planned to own:

- hosted cancellation flows
- retention coupons and discounts
- pause and downgrade paths
- machine-learning routing or adaptive offers
- no-code experimentation inside cancel flows

That means the straightforward version of RetainOS would spend too much time chasing feature parity instead of creating a real wedge.

## Key Competitor Reality

The strongest overlap came from:

- Stripe cancellation pages
- RevenueCat Customer Center
- Paddle Retain
- Chargebee Retention
- Churnkey Adaptive Offers

Churnkey in particular is close enough to the original thesis that building an "AI cancel-flow" product now would likely be wasted motion.

## What We Learned

The problem is real.

The Karpathy-style loop is real.

But the first product we picked is too crowded in its obvious form.

Good lesson:

- a real problem does not mean there is room for a new company in the obvious wedge
- a clean loop is not enough if distribution and product coverage are already owned
- category validation is useful, but it can also be a warning sign

## What We Are Not Doing

- no MVP build
- no landing page
- no design partner outreach for RetainOS in this form
- no more time on hosted cancel-flow productization

## Only Reason To Reopen

Reopen only if one of these is proven by real customer interviews:

- large customers want a vendor-independent control layer above Stripe, Paddle, Churnkey, Chargebee, or RevenueCat
- existing tools fail at long-horizon revenue-quality evaluation
- existing tools fail at enterprise policy, approval, or trust requirements
- there is a clear high-ACV gap that current vendors do not serve

Without one of those, this stays dead.

## Clean Next Move

Stop building in this folder.

Keep the notes as market research and move to the next idea with a cleaner wedge.
