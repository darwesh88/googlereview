# Validation Scorecard

Score each category from 1 to 5.

## Scoring Rubric

### Problem Severity

- `1`: Mild inconvenience, low urgency, infrequent pain
- `3`: Real friction, recurring problem, some urgency
- `5`: Acute recurring pain, costly or risky to ignore

### Buyer Clarity

- `1`: Vague audience, unclear user or payer
- `3`: Reasonable segment, some ambiguity around buyer
- `5`: Specific ICP, user, economic buyer, and trigger are clear

### Market Evidence

- `1`: Mostly speculation, little external proof
- `3`: Some complaints, alternatives, or visible demand signals
- `5`: Repeated strong signals from communities, spend, reviews, or known workflows

### Competitive Pressure

- `1`: Crowded market, little wedge, entrenched incumbents
- `3`: Competitive but some whitespace exists
- `5`: Weak direct competition or strong differentiated angle

### Distribution Feasibility

- `1`: No realistic path to first customers
- `3`: Reachable customers but acquisition path is uncertain
- `5`: Obvious channels, communities, outbound list, or founder advantage

### Monetization Willingness

- `1`: Hard to charge, weak ROI, unclear budget owner
- `3`: Some willingness to pay if positioned well
- `5`: Clear budget, obvious ROI, easy pricing narrative

### Build Complexity

- `1`: Complex product, many integrations, high trust burden
- `3`: Moderate complexity, MVP possible with focus
- `5`: Small wedge can deliver value quickly

### Validation Speed

- `1`: Hard to test quickly without full product
- `3`: Can validate with landing page, outreach, or manual workflow
- `5`: Can test demand in days with interviews or concierge offer

## Weighted Table Template

Use a table like this:

| Idea | Problem (20) | Buyer (15) | Evidence (15) | Competition (10) | Distribution (15) | Monetization (10) | Build (10) | Validation (5) | Total / 100 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Idea A | 4 | 5 | 3 | 3 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 5 | 79 |
| Idea B | 3 | 3 | 4 | 2 | 2 | 3 | 2 | 3 | 56 |

Multiply each score by its weight fraction:

- Problem: score x 4
- Buyer: score x 3
- Evidence: score x 3
- Competition: score x 2
- Distribution: score x 3
- Monetization: score x 2
- Build: score x 2
- Validation: score x 1

This converts 1 to 5 scores into a 100-point weighted score.

## Interpretation

- `80-100`: Strong candidate for immediate validation or build
- `65-79`: Promising but still assumption-heavy
- `50-64`: Interesting but weak first business
- `<50`: Do not build before major reframing
