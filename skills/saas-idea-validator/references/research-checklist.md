# Research Checklist

Use this checklist to avoid shallow idea analysis.

## Evidence To Look For

- Users openly complaining about the workflow
- Teams stitching together spreadsheets, Slack, email, Zapier, or agencies
- Buyers already paying for adjacent software or services
- Competitors with active pricing pages and customer language
- Job posts that reveal budget, workflow, or urgency
- Review-site complaints that expose feature gaps
- Founder access to a reachable niche or channel

## Useful Questions

- What event causes the buyer to start searching?
- What happens if they do nothing?
- What does the current workaround cost in time, risk, or money?
- Who signs off on spend?
- Can the first version be sold with one sentence?
- Can value be delivered manually before software is complete?

## Cheap Validation Experiments

- Interview 10 qualified buyers
- Pre-sell a manual service version
- Build a landing page with a focused CTA
- Run founder-led outbound to a tight ICP list
- Offer a concierge onboarding flow
- Prototype only the painful step, not the whole platform

## Warning Signs

- The idea sounds impressive but the buyer is fuzzy
- The main promise is "save time" with no concrete trigger
- The ICP is "small businesses", "creators", or another overly broad segment
- The product requires many features before it becomes useful
- The business depends on virality, marketplace liquidity, or heavy content at day one
- The user cannot explain why this should win over spreadsheets plus services
